Agricultural Exports from the United States on Credit

We facilitate U.S. agricultural exports using the USDA GSM-102 Export Credit Guarantee Program, allowing qualified international buyers to purchase American-origin agricultural products on deferred payment terms while U.S. sellers are paid immediately.

This structure uses non-funded Letters of Credit, government-backed guarantees, and U.S. confirming banks to reduce risk, ensure compliance, and enable scalable trade.

How Trade Finance Works (GSM-102 Process)

The U.S. Seller issues a Proforma Invoice / Proforma Contract to the Buyer.

The Buyer signs and accepts the Proforma Contract and returns it to the Seller.

The Seller submits the signed Proforma Contract and GSM-102 application directly to the USDA Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC).

The CCC issues a GSM-102 Guarantee Letter to the Seller.

The Seller requests a non-funded, open Letter of Credit (LC) from the Buyer.

The Buyer applies for the LC with its Issuing Bank.

The Issuing Bank issues the LC directly to the Seller.

The Seller ships U.S.-origin agricultural products to the Buyer.

The Seller negotiates the LC with a U.S. Confirming Bank.

The Confirming Bank pays the Seller at sight.

The Seller reports shipment completion and submits customs-certified proof of receipt to the CCC.

The Buyer repays the Issuing Bank under the deferred terms agreed in the contract.

The Issuing Bank repays the U.S. Confirming Bank under GSM-102 terms.

Only in the event of default, the CCC pays under the guarantee.

Government-Backed Credit Structure

Up to 98% of the invoice value, including freight, is guaranteed by the USDA Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC), significantly reducing payment risk for U.S. exporters and confirming banks.
