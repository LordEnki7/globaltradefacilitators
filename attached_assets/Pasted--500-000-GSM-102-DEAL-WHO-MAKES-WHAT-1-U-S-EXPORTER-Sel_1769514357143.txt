💼 $500,000 GSM-102 DEAL — WHO MAKES WHAT
1️⃣ U.S. EXPORTER (Seller of Goods)

Role: Supplies the product and gets paid at sight
Risk: Low (government-backed payment)

Typical Margin

8% – 15% depending on product

Dollar Range

Low: 8% → $40,000

Average: 10% → $50,000

Strong: 15% → $75,000

✅ Exporter gets paid immediately once documents are clean.

2️⃣ IMPORTER (Buyer / Distributor)

Role: Imports, distributes, sells locally
Risk: Medium (market + repayment obligation)

Typical Margin

12% – 25% (sometimes higher in Africa/Caribbean)

Dollar Range

Conservative: 12% → $60,000

Normal: 18% → $90,000

Aggressive: 25% → $125,000

⚠️ Importer carries:

Customs

VAT

FX exposure

Market risk

3️⃣ YOU — FACILITATOR / BROKER

Role: Structure, compliance, banks, LC, execution
Risk: Time + coordination (no inventory risk)

Typical Fee

1.5% – 3% of contract value

Often hybrid (setup + transaction)

Dollar Range

Conservative: $7,500

Standard: $10,000

Strong: $15,000

💡 Best structure:

$7,500–$10,000 upfront

1% embedded in transaction

4️⃣ U.S. BANK (Confirming / Financing Bank)

Role: Pays exporter, carries LC until repayment
Risk: Minimal (98% USDA guarantee)

Typical Earnings

0.75% – 1.5% annualized

Dollar Range

$3,750 – $7,500

Banks love this because:

Government guarantee

Short duration

Clean paper

5️⃣ FOREIGN BANK (Issuing Bank)

Role: Issues LC, finances importer locally
Risk: Importer credit risk

Typical Earnings

1% – 3% fees + interest

Dollar Range

$5,000 – $15,000

Often broken into:

LC issuance fee

Commitment fee

Interest spread

6️⃣ LOGISTICS / FREIGHT / INSURANCE

Role: Physical movement & risk coverage
Risk: Operational

Typical Cost (Not Profit)

4% – 8% of shipment

Dollar Range

$20,000 – $40,000

Paid by importer or embedded in CIF pricing.
